### Blind anecdotes

Core values interviews are similar to behavioral interviews, but more relaxed and less focused on your projects. They will ask questions like

- Tell me about a time you built something great from scratch
- What is something that you wanted to accomplish in the last year that you haven't accomplished yet?
- Tell me about an example of when you were part of a community

They will often have many follow up questions so make sure you know the story you're telling rather than just memorizing canned answers.

The interviewer hasn't read your resume and they don't care about the specifics of your projects. You don't need to sell your skills as a programmer or account manager. They want to know things like what motivates you, how you learn, how you deal with failures, etc.

Overall, it is an easy and casual interview. If you have prepared for behavioral interviews and know how to link your experiences to Airbnb's mission and core values, you will do just fine.

I had interviewed at Airbnb India last year and could not clear the cultural interviews. From the feedback that the recruiter shared with me, I understood the following things :

- First, make sure you understand the Airbnb values mentioned on their website clearly and have relevant examples for each.
- When they ask you a question, they expect your answer to highlight the above mentioned principles, even through they do not tell you that explicitly. So make sure all your examples are in line with the values.
- Also they ask you how Airbnb achieves or embodies the values or what people like about Airbnb in general. So make sure to go through their website and see recent examples of their work in various parts of the world and understand enough to be able to vomit it back out to the interviewer.
