- Practice, practice, PRACTICE
- Concentration is a process of distraction from less important matters
- Prepare first 3 sentences
- Have more confidence on the subject than the audience
- Pause a moment and look at your audience, a hundred chance to one they want you to succeed
- Do not make haste to begin, it shows lack of control
- Take a deep breath, relax and speak in a quite conversational tone as though you were talking to a large friend
- Like taking a child plunge: at you are in, the water is fine
- Monotony reveals our limitation
- The most important law of public speech is the necessity of truth, force, feeling and life.
- EMPHASIS is a matter of CONTRAST and COMPARISON
- Continual change of pitch is nature's highest method
- Change of tempo lends naturalness to the delivery
- Pause is silence made designedly eloquent
- Pausing after an important idea gives it time to penetrate
- A fault with public speakers is that they try to think of the succeeding sentence white still uttering the former
