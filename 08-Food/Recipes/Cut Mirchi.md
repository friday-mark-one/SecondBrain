## Ingredients
- [[Tamarind paste]] | 3 tbsp thick concentrate
- [[Cumin powder]] | 1 tsp
- [[Coriander powder]] | 1 tsp
- [[Jaggery]] | 1 tsp (or brown sugar)
- [[Bajji milakai]] | 6–8 (Anaheim peppers)
- [[Besan]] | 1 cup + 1 tsp
- [[Rice flour]] | 3 tbsp
- [[Corn starch]] | 1 tbsp
- [[Ajwain]] | ½ tsp
- [[Turmeric]] | ¼ tsp
- [[Baking soda]] | ¼ tsp
- [[Groundnut oil]] | 1 tbsp hot, for the batter
- [[Spray oil]] | for air-frying
- [[Chaat masala]] | to finish
- [[Onion]] | finely diced, to garnish
- [[Cilantro]] | to garnish
- [[Lemon]] | wedges, to serve

## Directions
1. Storable tamarind paste: mix 3 tbsp thick tamarind concentrate, 1 tsp cumin powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, ½ tsp salt, 1 tsp jaggery. Take out 1.5 tbsp for tonight and stir in 1 tsp besan to thicken; jar and refrigerate the rest.
2. Peppers: wash and fully dry 6–8 peppers. Make a single vertical slit in each (optional: scrape out seeds) and smear a thin layer of the thickened paste inside.
3. Batter: whisk 1 cup besan + 3 tbsp rice flour + 1 tbsp corn flour + ½ tsp ajwain + ¼ tsp turmeric + ¼ tsp baking soda + salt. Slowly whisk in ½–⅔ cup water to a thick, pancake-like batter; whisk in 1 tbsp hot oil at the end.
4. First fry (make-ahead): preheat air fryer to 375°F/190°C. Hold a pepper by the stem, dunk to coat heavily, place in the basket, and spray the tops generously with oil. Air fry 8–10 min, flipping halfway and spraying any dry flour spots. Cool 10–15 min, then slice into 1-inch rounds and hold in a container.
5. Final crunch (to serve): air fry at 400°F/200°C; spritz the cut sides with oil and fry 4–6 min until shatter-crisp and deep gold.
6. Serve: immediately toss with a heavy dusting of chaat masala; pile finely diced red onion and cilantro on top. Serve fresh with lemon wedges.
