## Ingredients
- [[White peas]] | 1 cup, soaked overnight
- [[Potato]] | 1, cubed
- [[Onion]] | 2, finely chopped
- [[Green chilli]] | 1, finely chopped
- [[Tomato]] | 1, finely chopped
- [[Cilantro]] | for garnish
- [[Turmeric]] | ¼ tsp
- [[Ginger garlic paste]] | 1 tsp
- [[Red chilli powder]] | ½ tsp
- [[Coriander powder]] | ½ tsp
- [[Cumin powder]] | ¼ tsp
- [[Garam masala]] | ¼ tsp
- [[Pani poori]] | for the base
- [[Sev]] | to top
- [[Chaat masala]] | to sprinkle

## Directions
**Prep**
1. Soak 1 cup white peas overnight.
2. Pressure-cook the soaked peas + 1 cubed potato with turmeric, salt and 3 cups water.

**Ragada**
3. Heat oil; add 1 finely chopped onion, 1 tsp ginger garlic paste and 1 finely chopped green chilli.
4. Once golden, add 1 chopped tomato.
5. Add ¼ tsp turmeric, ½ tsp chilli powder, ½ tsp coriander powder, ¼ tsp cumin powder, ¼ tsp garam masala and salt.
6. When oil separates, add the cooked peas + potato with their water. Boil 5 min, then mix and mash. Garnish with coriander.

**Assemble**
7. Break open pani pooris; place small cubes of boiled potato inside.
8. Add the ragada, then [[Green Chutney]] and [[Tamarind Chutney]].
9. Garnish with lots of finely chopped onion, coriander, sev and chaat masala.
