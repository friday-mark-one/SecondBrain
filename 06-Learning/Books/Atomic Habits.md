Changes that seem small and unimportant at first will compound into remarkable results if you are willing to stick with them for years

![[image-1653460936675.jpg6045997482977101844.jpg]]

Goals are about the results you want to achieve. Systems are about the process that lead to those results.

The purpose of setting goals is to win the game. The purpose of building systems is to continue playing the game. True long-term thinking is goal-less thinking. It's not about any single accomplishment. It is about the cycle of endless refinement and continuous improvement. Ultimately, it is your commitment to the process that will determine your progress.

![[image-1653461597998.jpg596458213477531253.jpg]]

Outcomes are about what you get. Processes are about what you do. Identity is about what you believe.

The ultimate form of intrinsic motivation is when habit becomes part of your identity it's one thing to say and the type of person who wants this. It's something very different to say I'm the type of person who is this.

If you're proud of the size of your biceps you'll make sure you never skip an upper body workout. Once your pride gets involved, you'll fight tooth and nail to maintain your habits.

1. Decide the type of person you want to be 
2. Prove it to yourself with small wins

Rewards are the end goal of every habit. The cue is about noticing the reward. The craving is about wanting the reward. The response is about obtaining the reward.

You do not tell your hair to grow, your heart to pump, your lungs to breathe, or your stomach to digest. And yet your body handles all this and more on autopilot. You are much more than your conscious self.

The format for creating an **implementation intention** is: “When situation X arises, I will perform response Y.”

Being specific about what you want and how you will achieve it helps you say no to things that derail progress, distract your attention, and pull you off course.

**Habit stacking **is a special form of implementation intention. Rather than pairing your new habit with a particular time and location, your pair it with a current habit.

It is easier to build new habits in a new environment because you are not fighting against old cues.
