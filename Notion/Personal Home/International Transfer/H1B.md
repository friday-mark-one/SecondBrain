**What do I put for the question, “Has an immigrant petition ever filed on your behalf?”
**There are three (3) instances where you would answer “Yes” to this question: 1) If an I-140 (or the second stage of the permanent residence process) has been filed on your behalf, 2) If an I-130 (or family based green card) has been filed on your behalf, or 3) If an I-360 (or petition for specific categories of immigrants like widowers, abused parents, abused spouses, religious workers, US armed forces members, etc.) has been filed on your behalf. You may include an I-140, I-130, or I-360 receipt number, the name of the petitioner, and priority date as additional information, if applicable. Otherwise, your answer would be “No.”

### Sample approval email

The consular officer has approved your application subject to final processing by the consular section that is necessary to issue your visa. When that processing is complete, the status will change to "Issued". However, a final screening step must still be completed prior to issuance that could lead a consular officer to conclude that you are not eligible for a visa. If that occurs, the consular officer will notify you that your application has been denied and your status will change to "Refused”
