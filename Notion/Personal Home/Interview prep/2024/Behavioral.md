**Introduce yourself**

I am Bharath and I’ve spent the past 8 years building backend software for Amazon Kindle that directly impacts millions of users. 

My initial stint was in the Kindle Simulator team in Chennai, where I worked on a cross-platform desktop application, built with C++ and Qt framework, that allows authors to create and proof-read high-quality e-books. 

Next, I worked in the Kindle ingestion service that processes thousands of books per minute. This is where I developed experience with AWS services like S3, lambda, DynamoDB, Redshift, SWF and a lot more. 

Now, for the past 4 years, I’ve led a few complex projects in the Kindle rendering engine team. Redesigned a legacy E-ink waveform architecture, which uses Skia framework, and it improved page turn performance and reduced ghosting.

My current project is extending the infrastructure for AI features to be delivered to each device.
