### Major entry signals

- Breakout of a trading range
- Breakout of a 1-2-3 high/low
- Breakout of a Ross hook


### Things to note

- Use daily-weekly chart relationship or intraday-daily chart relationship
- Ignore all gap breakouts

### Breakout of a trading range

- A trading range will be preceded on the daily chart by either a large gap or a day which is large in size from high to low.
- Wait for trading range to evolve
- There will be a leg counter to the thrust of the gap or large day move.
- Then there will be a second leg in the direction of the gap or large day move. So it will look like this /\ or \/. Now draw a horizontal line across the highest high and the lowest low. This will take 10 days.
- Then a third leg forms - /\/ or \/\. Redraw the horizontal lines unless they are already intact.
- Now count the number of the bars in the daily chart. Between 22 - 25, fourth leg will be completed - /\/\ or \/\/. Redraw the envelope, but usually not necessary.
- If the number approaches the proximity of the envelope and makes a non-gap breakout, enter the trade.
- Least frequent occurrence but best to make big profit.

- Point 2 is higher than 1
- Market needs to trade through the entry point not gap over
- This pattern occurs only at market turning points. i.e. when a market appears to make a top or has reached 50% or greater retracement.
