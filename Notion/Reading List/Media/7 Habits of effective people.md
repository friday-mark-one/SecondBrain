## Inside out

The more aware we are of our basic paradigms, maps, or assumptions, and the extent to which we have been influenced by our experience, the more we can take responsibility for those paradigms, examine them, test them against reality, listen to others and be open to their perceptions, thereby getting a larger picture and a far more objective law - PARADIGM SHIFT

Borrowing strength builds weakness because it reinforces dependence on external factors to get things done.

## 7 Habits an overview

We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit.

Habit is the intersection of knowledge, skill and desire.

Knowledge - The theoretical paradigm, the what to do and the why

Skill - the how to do

Desire - The motivation, the want to do

Dependence is the paradigm of YOU

Independence is the paradigm of I

Interdependence is the paradigm of WE

P/PC balance

P - production of desired results

PC - Production capability

Excessive focus on P results in ruined health, worn-out machines, depleted bank accounts

Too much focus on PC is like a person who runs three or four hours a day, bragging about the extra ten years of life it creates, unaware he’s spending them running

To maintain the P/PC balance is often a difficult judgment call.

## Habit 1: Be Proactive

Try to stand apart from yourself

3 social maps

- Genetic determinism
- Psychic determinism
- Environmental determinism

Between stimulus and response is our greatest power - the freedom to choose.

**Definition**: Our behaviour is a function of our decisions, not our conditions. Reactive people are affected by their physical environment.

When someone takes an irresponsible position and waits for someone else to make things happen, we tell them "Use your R & I" (Resourcefulness and Initiative)

Holding people to the responsible course is not demeaning; it is affirming.

**Language of proactivity**

I choose to go to the tennis trip vs I have to go to the tennis trip

Love is a verb and not a feeling.

Circle of concern filled with HAVE

- I'll be happy when I HAVE my house paid off.
- If I HAD my degree...

Circle of influence filled with BE

- I can BE more patient
- I can BE wise

Learn from mistakes proactively

**Making and keeping commitments**

We can make a promise - and keep it.

We can set a goal - and work to achieve it

This gives us an awareness of self control and courage of strength to accept more responsibility

## Habit 3: Put first things first

*Question 1: What one thing could you do*

*(something you aren't doing now) that, if you did*

*it on a regular basis, would make a tremendous*

*positive difference in your personal life?*

**Answer:**

- Concentrate on healthy practices like meditation, healthy diet.

*Question 2: What one thing in your business or*

*professional life would bring similar results?*

**Answer:**

- Steal the experience of others by understanding their works and the subtleties involved.
- Practice coding problems.

The one denominator that all successful people share is Habit 3.

Best thinking in the area of time management can be captured in a single phrase: **Organize and execute around priorities.**

- First generation - Notes and checklists
- Second generation - Calendar and appointment
- Third generation - Time management and prioritization
    - Often rejected because of compromise in quality of life and strict schedule.
- Fourth generation - Focus on preserving and enhancing relationships and accomplishing results.

**Urgent vs Important**

Urgent is now and can be useless.

Importance has to do with results, that contributes to mission, values and priority goals.

**Quadrant 1** - Urgent and important

- Keep only genuinely urgent things here

**Quadrant 2** - Not Urgent but important

- Effective people stay here. It deals with things like personal relationships, writing personal mission statement, exercising and long-range planning. These people also shrink Quadrant 1 down to size by spending more time here.

**Quadrant 3** - Urgent but not important

- People here think these are important by reacting to crisis and expectations of others

**Quadrant 4** - Not urgent and not important

- Irresponsible people

Effective people staying in quadrant 2 keep P and PC balance by focusing on the important but not urgent.

What it takes to say no

- You can only increase work in Quadrant 2 by saying no to things from other quadrants based on actual priorities and values.
- You should have a burning yes to something which should help you to say no.
- To have a burning yes, you should have a solid habit 2 - A personal mission statement.
