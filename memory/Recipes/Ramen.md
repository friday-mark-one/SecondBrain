## Ingredients
- [[Tofu]] | extra firm, ~½ block, cubed
- [[Corn flour]] | 3 tbsp, to coat the tofu
- [[Groundnut oil]] | for sautéing
- [[Bok choy]] | 2 stalks
- [[Shiitake Mushroom]] | a handful, sliced
- [[Broccoli]] | as needed
- [[Spring onion]] | a little
- [[Garlic]] | 5–7 cloves
- [[Ginger]] | equal amount to the garlic
- [[Gochujang]] | 2 tbsp
- [[Chilli garlic oil]] | 2–3 tsp
- [[Veg stock]] | 2 cups
- [[Coconut milk]] | 1 cup
- [[Peanut butter]] | 1 tbsp
- [[Soy sauce]] | to taste
- [[Guan miao noodles]] | thin, from T&T
- [[Sesame seeds]] | to garnish

## Directions
1. Tofu: cube ~½ block extra-firm tofu; toss with 3 tbsp corn flour and salt. Sauté in groundnut oil until golden; set aside.
2. Veg (same pan): sauté the bok choy, stems and leaves separately. Sauté sliced shiitake until browned, then soak in soy sauce. Boil the broccoli, then sauté. Set all aside.
3. Broth: sauté fine-chopped garlic (5–7 cloves), an equal amount of ginger, and the spring onion bulbs in groundnut oil. Stir in 2 tbsp gochujang and 2 tsp chilli garlic oil (more to taste). Add 2 cups veg stock and 1 cup coconut milk; add a little salt and simmer.
4. Paste: mix 1 tbsp peanut butter, 1 tsp chilli garlic oil, and soy sauce into a creamy paste.
5. Assemble: divide the paste between 2 bowls, pour in the broth, and check salt (adjust soy sauce / peanut butter to taste).
6. Noodles: boil the guanmiao noodles for 2 min, then add to the bowls. Top with tofu, broccoli, bok choy, spring onion, mushrooms, and sesame seeds.
